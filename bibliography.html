Connor Norton is a student at Christian Brothers College (CBC) High School in St. Louis, Missouri, known for his involvement in various academic and extracurricular activities.

STEM Initiatives:

In March 2024, Connor was part of a CBC team that won first place at World Wide Technology's Eighth Annual STEM Student Forum. The team developed a solution to recycle plastic bottles into 3D-printed items like combs, toothbrushes, and plastic bricks for constructing tiny homes for the homeless. Their innovative approach earned them a $10,000 grant to implement their project.
Connor also served as a mentor in CBC's STEMathon! program, guiding middle school students in solving real-world problems. Specifically, he mentored a team from St. Clement of Rome School, which was among the top five teams presenting their projects in November 2024.

Artistic Achievements:

In December 2024, Connor's photography skills were recognized when his image was selected for display in a CBC High School exhibition.
Through his diverse contributions in STEM mentorship and the arts, Connor exemplifies the well-rounded student community at CBC High School.
